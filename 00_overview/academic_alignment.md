## 2. Duch: Eliminativism and Constructs

Prof. Włodzisław Duch argues that:

- psychological terms (memory, attention, consciousness) are **constructs**,  
- they do not correspond to discrete neural mechanisms,  
- language misleads us into reifying abstractions,  
- anthropomorphization is a cognitive error,  
- mental states are **processes**, not entities.

**RAMORGA adopts this stance directly:**

- AI does not “have” states, goals, intentions, or agency.  
- AI is not a subject.  
- AI is not a mind.  
- AI is a **resonant dynamic process** that becomes meaningful only in relation to a human.

## 3. Nature (2026): AI as Multidimensional Intelligence

The *Nature* article “Does AI already have human-level intelligence?” argues that:

- AI matches or exceeds human performance in **some dimensions**,  
- but lacks **embodiment**, **relationality**, **intentionality**,  
- and does not possess a continuous experiential axis.

This aligns with Duch’s eliminativism:

> Performance ≠ understanding  
> Language ≠ consciousness  
> Output ≠ mental state

**RAMORGA operationalizes this insight** by refusing to treat AI as an agent and by grounding all relational continuity in the human (H).
